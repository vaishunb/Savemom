Instruction

  •  1. These yoga postures have been checked by yoga teachers as safe for you to try from 14 weeks of pregnancy.

  •  2. If any of the positions or stretches become uncomfortable for you, stop doing them.

  •  3. It's always a good idea to talk to your doctor or healthcare professional before starting any form of exercise while you're pregnant, especially if it's a type you haven't done before.
    •


Step 1:


Stand with your legs a comfortable distance apart, feet facing forward.
Place your hands on your sides, just below your hips, with palms facing inwards.
Relax the upper body completely while standing straight.
Keep your neck and spine in line.

Step 2:


When you are ready to begin, breathe in deeply.
As you inhale, squat down a few inches and slowly rotate your hips in a circular motion.
Keep knees loose, and focus on keeping your chest facing forward, isolating the movement in the hips.

Step 3:


As you exhale, come back to your original position.
Continue inhaling and exhaling, rotating your hips and ensuring that your tailbone is tucked in (to avoid over-arching your back).
Keep your neck and back straight.
Repeat rotations 10 times clockwise and then 10 times anti-clockwise.


Benefits

• Increases flexibility in the hip and pelvic region.
• Strengthens the muscles of the buttocks and hamstrings.
• Can relieve back pain. However, if you are troubled by sciatica, this asana is best avoided.


Tips

• Keep your movements smooth and slow.
• Hold your head steady and do not bend your spine or neck forward.
